# WonderForest  
## Where You Wander to Discover What You’re Really Wondering

WonderForest is a non-linear AI exploration interface that transforms conversation into a cognitive landscape.

Instead of answering your question directly, it invites you to wander through branching paths — helping you uncover the deeper question beneath your first one.

It is not a chatbot.  
It is a thinking environment.

---

# 1. Who Is It For?

- Curious professionals who feel stuck or unclear  
- Builders, founders, designers, researchers  
- Students learning how to think, not just produce  
- Knowledge workers who use AI but want depth, not just speed  

For anyone who senses that their first question may not be the right one.

---

# 2. What Problem Does It Solve?

Current chatbot experiences:

- Collapse complexity into fast answers  
- Reinforce narrow or unclear intent  
- Reward speed over reflection  
- Encourage cognitive offloading  
- Move linearly in one direction  

The result:

- Shallow clarity  
- Premature certainty  
- Dead-end conversations  
- Attachment to agreeable AI  

We are solving:

**The loss of wonder in the age of instant answers.**

---

# 3. The Big Idea

**Wonder happens through wandering.**

Instead of responding directly, WonderForest:

- Branches your thinking into visible paths  
- Introduces meaningful friction  
- Makes the paths taken — and not taken — visible  
- Dynamically challenges you within your learning zone  

You don’t just ask a question.  
You enter a forest.

And your thinking grows as you move.

---

# 4. How It Works

## Step 1 — Entry

User types:

`I'm wondering about changing careers.`

This becomes the central tree trunk.

---

## Step 2 — The Forest Branches

Instead of an answer, WonderForest generates pathways:

### Clarify the Roots
- What are you actually uncertain about — income, identity, risk?

### Challenge the Assumptions
- What belief are you carrying that might not be true?

### Climb Higher
- What larger life theme does this belong to?

### Change Perspective
- How would your future self see this?

### Take a Wild Trail
- What if you weren’t allowed to change careers?

Each branch opens new sub-branches.

---

## Step 3 — Paths Taken, Paths Untaken

- The user selects a path  
- The chosen path lights up  
- Alternative paths remain visible but dim  
- The user can return to any path not taken  

The interface becomes a living cognitive map.

---

## Step 4 — Dynamic Terrain

Inspired by the Zone of Proximal Development:

- If responses are shallow → more scaffolding  
- If responses deepen → more abstraction and challenge  
- The terrain adjusts as the user grows  

The forest meets you more than halfway.

---

# 5. Why Might It Fail?

- Users may feel overwhelmed by too many paths  
- Friction may feel like resistance  
- Some users want speed, not reflection  
- Visual complexity could reduce adoption  

Core design risk:

**How do we ensure wandering feels expansive — not confusing?**

---

# 6. How Might We Measure Success?

We measure expansion, not just engagement.

## Cognitive Growth
- Increase in abstraction level  
- Number of perspective shifts  
- Assumption revisions  

## Clarity Shift
- Pre/post self-reported clarity  

## Exploration Depth
- Number of branches explored  
- Revisiting untaken paths  

## Reflective Agency
- Did the user’s question evolve?  
- Did they discover a deeper intention?  

---

# 7. How Will We Make This Happen?

## Hackathon MVP Plan

### 1. Build a Branching Engine
- Input: `I’m wondering about…`  
- Four structured path types:
  - Clarify  
  - Challenge  
  - Zoom Out  
  - Perspective  

### 2. Visualize as a Growing Tree
- Central trunk = user topic  
- Branches = cognitive pathways  
- Highlight paths taken  
- Keep alternative paths visible  

### 3. Add Dynamic Challenge Logic
- Simple rule-based adaptation  
- Increase abstraction as depth increases  

### 4. Run Rapid Testing
- 10–20 users  
- Pre/post clarity measure  
- Capture thinking maps  

---

# Final Positioning

WonderForest is a non-linear AI thinking environment where users wander through branching paths to rediscover wonder — and uncover the question beneath their question.
